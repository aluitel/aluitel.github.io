@ Microsoft

At Microsoft, I led product strategy for Security Copilot, the first generative AI-powered security platform. My work focused on responsible AI, privacy, sovereign cloud enablement, and compliance—empowering government and regulated sectors to adopt the product and safeguard the public interest at machine speed.

In parallel, I led the TPM strategy for Microsoft’s Secure Future Initiative (SFI) within the Cloud Ecosystem Security division, spanning more than ten product lines. I helped embed secure-by-design development practices, improve engineering maturity, and align execution with Microsoft’s global software assurance strategy and readiness for AI-era risks.

This role required deep cross-functional collaboration—connecting engineering, legal, compliance, and executive stakeholders—to ensure security was not an afterthought, but a foundation.

@ Active Duty Military

Proudly served in the world’s finest Navy under high-pressure conditions, gaining cross-functional experience, leadership skills, and operational discipline—while embodying a mission-ready mindset rooted in honor, courage, and integrity.

As a young Sailor stationed aboard the USS Bonhomme Richard in the vast waters of the South China Sea, I learned to lead and endure in one of the world’s most demanding environments. Early mornings, long nights, and the steady hum of a warship became my rhythm. Every day brought new challenges, and I met each with humility and resolve.

Coming from a background where military service was not the traditional path, the decision to enlist was bold and deeply personal. There was no roadmap—only a conviction to serve, grow, and pursue a purpose greater than myself.

This journey asked for more than physical endurance. It required sacrifice. I missed birthdays and milestones. I lost touch with people I once held close. Some connections faded; others were lost entirely. But the experience gave me something far greater—a sense of duty, a global perspective, and an unshakable foundation of resilience.

Serving in the Navy shaped not only my leadership but my character. It taught me to navigate ambiguity, build trust across cultures, and lead in moments that demanded calm under pressure.

This is where I learned what it truly means to serve—with intention, with integrity, and with impact.
